Business Professionals of America is a student organization of more than 50,000 members from 2,700 chapters nationwide whose aim is to promote leadership and professionalism for the next generation to enter the workforce. Since 1998, the Carlson chapter of BPA has been involved


Business Profesionals of America was started in 1963 when Vocational Education was established. Business Professionals of America stands for many different things. Business stands for the field for which we prepare our students; emphasizes that we educate our members to work efficiently, not only in an office setting, but also in a wide variety of business situations. Professionals means that our students indicate they join BPA to take advantage of a wide variety of professional development opportunities. Last but not least America symbolizes pride in our country and its free enterprise business system.


The Emblem of the Business Professionals of America is a shield. the four points on the shield stand for Ambition, Leadership, Sociability, and Poise.The shield itself stands for honor and dignity and the importance of business in America. The stripes represent education, citizenship, loyalty, patriotism, competency, and dependability. The bar represents the service provided by employees in business occupations. The quill and inkwell represent the stability of business occupations through the ages, and the torch represents worthy goals. The official colors of the Business Professionals of America are Navy blue and Red


Business Profesionals of America is organized into many different divisions. you have the College/ Post-secondary Division, High School/ Secondary Division, Middle Level division, the Alumni Division, and the Professional Membership. The College / Post-secondary Division is for post-secondary students enrolled or formerly enrolled in initial, refresher, or upgrading business, career technical, business education and career or related education programs. The High School/ Secondary Division is for high school students enrolled or formerly enrolled in initial, refresher, or upgrading business, career technical, and career or related education programs. Associate Member Designation is for high school students enrolled or formerly enrolled in initial, refresher, or upgrading business, career technical, and career or related education programs for special populations. The Middle Level Division is for middle school students enrolled or formerly enrolled in initial, refresher, or upgrading business, career technical, and career or related education programs. The Alumni Division is for former active members of any student division. Last but not least is the Professional Membership. A professional member may be a person associated with BPA. Professional members are stand-alone members, not serving as part of a chartered division.


From sponsorships and scholarships to workshops, competition judging and exhibitor opportunities, businesses and organization can help shape and support our future business professionals through a variety of programs and services conducted throughout the year. Each engagement opportunity helps motivate students toward real-world career paths whether government, public, private or nonprofit. The success of Business Professionals of America is rooted in its many experiential programs which provide our students with an innovative approach to learning. Businesses and organizations that partner with BPA have a unique opportunity to participate in and influence these programs every day. BPA recognizes the value and benefit these relationships bring to our student members, educators and to our organization. It is through the support of our partners that Business Professionals of America is able to continue developing programs which enhance leadership skills and offer educational enrichment preparing out students for promising careers.
